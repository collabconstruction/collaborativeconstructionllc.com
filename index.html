Massachusetts' new ADU law

Massachusetts' new Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) law, part of the Affordable Homes Act signed in August 2024, went into effect on February 2, 2025, requiring municipalities to allow ADUs by right in most single-family residential districts. This significant policy shift aims to address the state's housing shortage by simplifying the approval process and providing homeowners with flexible options, such as accommodating aging parents or generating rental income. The law allows for both attached and detached units, subject to certain statewide standards and local restrictions.

We specialize in building ADUs and home additions.
